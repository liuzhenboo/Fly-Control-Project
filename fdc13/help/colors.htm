Color conventions for the FDC toolbox

In the blockdiagrams from the FDC toolbox, all 'normal' function blocks have standard colors: black foreground, white background, no shadows. Shadows have been used for Inports and Outports (which makes it easier to distinguish them from Constant blocks) and for 'Button'-blocks, which start MATLAB programs or execute MATLAB commands when being double-clicked. The artificial feedback loops from the system Beaver, including the masked subsystem Hlpfcn, are magenta, in order to distinguish these signals from the external inputs and outputs which enter the system Beaver by means of Inport blocks. Title blocks on the upper left side of the (sub-)systems use a blue foreground on a white background. The title-blocks that will display help texts when being double-clicked are distinguished by their shadows.

A complete list of color schemes has been given below.

In the autopilot simulation systems APILOT1, APILOT2, and APILOT3, the following color conventions have been used:


Signals used for gain-scheduling (notably the airspeed V and altitude H)Magenta on white with shadows for the Inport/Outport blocks of these signals
Reference signals, issued directly by the subsystem block Reference Signals and switch-settings from the block Mode ControllerRed on white with shadows for the Inport/Outport blocks of these signals
Reference signals which are sent to the inner-loops by the outer-loopsBlack on orange with shadows for the Inport/Outport blocks of these signals
To Workspace blocksBlack on yellow, no shadows
S-function blocks and subsystems that can also be used as stand-alone systems (in particular: links to the non-linear aircraft model Beaver)Black on light blue, no shadows
